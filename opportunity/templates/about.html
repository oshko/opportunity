Seeking a job

Most of us at one time or another need to go out and find a job. Sometimes it is easy, but often it is a challenge. In these difficult economic times, it is harder. This site is dedicated to helping job seekers.

There are many good resources online. The combination of LinkedIn and Meetup are great for business networking. There are job boards and aggregation sites which allow you find job openings at many companies.

It is common for companies to use computer systems to track their interactions with job seekers and filter resumes. What if there was a system for job seeker?

A dashboard from which one could track interactions (e.g., applied for a position, spoke with someone, interviewer with some group) with companies. Investigating what is important to them to allow you to put together a value proposition and hone your elevator pitch.


Social networks are a household word today. One of the seminal works that lead to these social networks is The Strength of Weak Ties

by Mark S. Granovetter

He was a PhD student at John Hopkins University studying how poeple found jobs. Don't go job hunting alone! Today's social networks are big on sharing. The word share appears in Granovetter's paper twice. Leveraging your social network is essential on the job hunt but there is a time when you want to confide in your family, close friends or a mentor. This website can help with that. By default, you nothing is shared. When you want to get some feedback from close associates, you can share your portfolio with them.
